title: "April 2026 Training Analysis"
---

April started with an FTP test on day one. Not as a statement — as a tool. After three months of base building and a four-week threshold block, it was time to see where the fitness actually sat. The result would recalibrate every bike training zone for the rest of the season.

From there, April became the sharpest month so far: fartlek runs, Z4/Z5 bike intervals, 4-hour mixed-terrain rides, brick sessions off long bikes. The CTL hit 133 by month end, and the training looked less like "building toward Lake Placid" and more like "getting ready to race."

---

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|

| Total training hours | ~62h |
| Total distance | ~1,391 km |
| Total TSS | 4,387 |
| End-of-month CTL | 133.6 |

51 sessions is the highest single-month count of the year. Total hours were slightly below March, but TSS was higher — a sign that the sessions were more intense, not just longer. The negative TSB (-20.1) at month end reflects a high-load April with real fatigue accumulated, which is exactly where you want to be eight weeks before a 70.3 tune-up.

## Swim

April's swim work reflected the sharper overall training theme — 13 sessions, the highest monthly swim volume of the year so far:

- **Speed and quality sets**: 12×150m varying intensity (Apr 10, Apr 22), Força e Velocidade (Apr 15, Apr 22) — short fast efforts training the top end of the stroke
- **Velocidade + A1** (Apr 8): speed work paired with aerobic maintenance — sharpening without burning out
- **A1 and A2 sessions**: consistent technical/aerobic base throughout the month, maintaining feel across the heavier bike and run load
- **Equipment sets** (Sessão Equipamento): paddles and pull buoy work for upper-body strength and stroke mechanics
- **Progressivo A1→A2→A3** (Apr 24): the month closed with a progressive effort set, a good signal that swim fitness was building

By the end of April, swim frequency was consistent and the quality was improving — a discipline actively being developed rather than just maintained.

---

## Bike

April's bike sessions pushed into territory the earlier months hadn't reached:

- **FTP test on April 1**: the benchmark that recalibrated everything. A max-effort 20-minute test followed by a cool-down. The number this produces sets all Z4/Z5 targets for the rest of the season.
- **Z4/Z5 interval blocks**: multiple sessions labeled "Semana 1: Z4/Z5," "Bloco em Z4/Z5," "Sequencia em Z4/Z5" — sustained efforts above threshold, building the ability to sustain race-relevant power
- **Mixed terrain rides** (4+ hours): Apr 5 (120 km), Apr 11 (150 km), Apr 25 (150 km) — long weekend rides with varied zones rather than flat Z2 cruise. These are the hardest training days of the month
- **Z4 BIG GEAR** (Apr 30): the final session of the month pushed both strength and threshold — big gear grinding at high effort. Specific prep for Lake Placid's climbs
- **Brick sessions**: Apr 25 paired a 150 km, 4-hour ride with an immediate 11 km run — the longest brick of the year. Running well off a four-hour bike ride is a skill, and April was when it started getting trained seriously

The Z4/Z5 work is what separates April from the previous months. January through March built the aerobic ceiling; April started pushing through it.

---

## Run

April's run was the most demanding of the year:

- **Fartlek sessions** (Fartlek 300m/200m, Fartlek 200m/200m + Zona 2): alternating fast and easy intervals — one of the most effective ways to build running speed without the injury risk of pure track work
- **6×1,200m Z4** (Apr 12, Apr 26): six reps of 1,200m at Z4 pace — the sharpest run sessions of the year. Threshold run repeats of this length build the specific lactate tolerance that holds Ironman run pace together in the back half of the marathon
- **Força e Velocidade** run sessions: short, sharp neuromuscular work — training the legs to turn over quickly
- **Brick runs**: Apr 25 brick run off the long bike — specific Ironman race simulation
- **Easy recovery runs**: multiple "Ritmo Fácil," "Corridinha Fácil" sessions keeping frequency high without digging fatigue holes

The fartlek pattern is worth calling out. Rather than classic track intervals (all hard, then all rest), fartlek keeps you moving through the recovery — teaching the body to recover while still running, which is much closer to what actually happens in a race.

---

## Strength

Five strength sessions in April — consistent with the monthly pattern. The key is that the sessions continued even as bike and run intensity peaked. That discipline is what keeps the structural integrity intact when the load is highest.

---

## Fitness Metrics at End of Month

CTL at 133.6 puts the fitness well into the range needed for a competitive Ironman. The progression across four months — 79 → 103 → 119 → 134 — is steady and purposeful. Nothing was rushed; each month built on the last.

The ATL at 155.6 (higher than CTL) and TSB at -20 reflects a body in active training stress. May will need to manage this carefully — volume up, but fatigue kept from spiraling.

---

## What's Next

May is the final volume block before the Rockford 70.3 on June 14. The work shifts back toward endurance — longer bikes, longer runs — while keeping some intensity in the schedule. The goal is to peak CTL around 140 and then let TSB recover into positive territory before the race. After Rockford, the final build toward Lake Placid begins.
