title: "January 2026 Training Analysis"
---

This is the first in a series of monthly training analysis posts. I look back at what actually happened — the numbers, the sessions, and what they mean for the bigger picture. The goal isn't just to log data. It's to understand patterns, catch drift early, and hold myself accountable to the process.

January 2026 was a base-building month. No target race on the immediate horizon, but Ironman Lake Placid on July 19 is the anchor of the entire year. Everything before it is construction.

---

## Overall Stats

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Completed sessions | 38 |
| Total training hours | ~47h |
| Total distance | ~1,204 km |
| Total TSS | 2,664 |
| End-of-month CTL | 79.2 |
| End-of-month ATL | 50.6 |
| End-of-month TSB | +32.8 |

38 sessions across 31 days is solid but not maxed out. Several days had just one session, and some lighter days were built in. The load is real but controlled.

---

## Weekly Load Progression

| Week | TSS |
|------|-----|
| Dec 29–Jan 4 | 469 |
| Jan 5–11 | 705 |
| Jan 12–18 | 827 |
| Jan 19–25 | 491 |
| Jan 26–31 | 174 |

A sharp build through week 3, then a significant drop in weeks 4–5. The last week of January was a recovery week — only Zwift rides and a couple of easy sessions. That's intentional. It's how you absorb a load block without accumulating chronic fatigue.

---

## Swim

January's swim volume was light — a few technique-focused sessions, including parachute drills and A1-paced sets. These aren't the sessions that build big swim fitness. They keep feel in the water and maintain technical quality when volume is low elsewhere.

The swim is always the discipline with the most room for improvement, and January wasn't the month to chase it. Setting the aerobic base on bike and run first is the right priority at this stage.

---

## Bike

Cycling drove the bulk of the load, as it should in a base phase. The mix:

- **Zwift Z2 rides** (Tempus Fugit in Watopia — flat, controlled): honest aerobic work without terrain dictating effort
- **Weekend long rides**: 120 km on Jan 3, 140 km on Jan 10, 132 km on Jan 17 — the backbone of the base block
- **Shorter Z2 sessions mid-week**: keeping the aerobic engine turning without heavy fatigue

RPM and intensity stayed low and deliberate throughout. Base is base — the point is time at aerobic effort, not heroics.

---

## Run

Run volume was modest in January, which is appropriate. The sessions labeled "Base 2026" and "Intensidade bem Fácil, FC baixa" (very easy, low heart rate) say it clearly: this was aerobic conditioning, not development work.

A 14 km run on Jan 11 at a comfortable pace, and a couple of shorter mid-week efforts. No intervals, no tempo. The goal was frequency and easy miles to rebuild the base that Ironman run prep will demand later.

---

## Strength

Two strength sessions in January — "Treino A - BASE" and "Treino B - BASE." Maintenance volume, not development. At this stage of the year, showing up and doing the work is the goal. Building structural resilience early makes the high-intensity months later safer.

---

## Fitness Metrics at End of Month

A CTL of 79 at the end of January is a reasonable starting point for an athlete building toward an Ironman in July. The high TSB (+32.8) is the signature of a month that ended with a recovery week — the body is fresh, and the fitness accumulated in weeks 2–3 is being absorbed.

The CTL will build from here.

---

## What's Next

February is where the real base building begins. The plan is to increase weekly hours, keep intensity mostly aerobic, and start building the consistent multi-sport routine that will carry through spring. CTL needs to climb steadily toward the 120–140 range by May to be on track for Lake Placid.
