March was defined by a four-week Z3/Z4 intensity block that started in the final days of February. Four consecutive weeks of structured bike intervals, progressive run sessions, and long weekend rides — all while keeping swim frequency consistent. By the end of the month the body was absorbing serious load, and the CTL kept climbing.

There was also a half marathon somewhere in there. "Meia Chegando" (half marathon coming) appeared as a session tag mid-month, suggesting a race or time trial that added real intensity to an already demanding block. And at the end of March, max-effort swim tests — 200m and 400m all-out efforts. The data-gathering phase.

## Swim

March's swim stayed aerobic while the bike and run carried the intensity block — which is correct periodization. Piling Z3/Z4 swim work on top of a hard bike/run phase risks cumulative fatigue without proportional return.

- **A1 and A2 maintenance sets**: the bulk of swim sessions stayed aerobic — consistent water time without digging a fatigue hole
- **Lap swimming sessions** (mid-week): volume work to keep feel and technique sharp
- **Progressivo A1→A2→A3**: progressive effort sets building through the zones, reinforcing pace sense
- **Sessão Equipamento**: paddles and pull buoy for upper-body strength and stroke mechanics
- **Max effort tests** at month end: 1×200m and 1×400m all-out — the first benchmark tests of the year

The tests are meaningful. Knowing your 200m and 400m times gives you calibrated training zones and tells you where you stand relative to race-pace targets. Coming after a hard March rather than a fresh block makes them conservatively honest.

## Bike

The bike was where the Z3/Z4 block lived:

- **BLOCO Z3>Z4 (semanas 2, 3, 4 de 4)**: weekly interval sessions progressively increasing in duration and intensity
- **Long weekend rides** anchoring volume: 161 km on Mar 7, 162 km on Mar 14, 129 km on Mar 22 — sustained aerobic riding building endurance beneath the intervals
- **Força Específica e Zona 3**: specific strength-endurance work — not just aerobic cruise, but sustained Zone 3 effort for extended periods
- **Brick combos**: Mar 28 paired a 130 km Z3 bike with an immediate T2 run — the most race-specific session of the month
- **Big gear subidas** (weeks 2, 3, 4): hill-strength work from February continued with progressive increases in rep duration (7×35s → 7×40s → 7×50s)

Going from 6×30s in February to 7×50s in March week 4 is a meaningful increase in specific muscular load. Lake Placid demands this.

---

## Run

March's running told the clearest story of the month:

- **Long aerobic runs** building to 22–23 km: the Mar 8 "Longo em Pace Fácil" and Mar 15 "Rodando em Ritmo Super Tranquilo" were genuine long runs — sustained Z1/Z2 efforts building time on feet
- **Weekly interval progression**: each week's running session pushed slightly harder — from Z2/Z3 combinations toward full Z4 efforts by week 4
- **Meia Chegando** (half marathon coming) on Mar 11: likely a race or prep session that added a harder effort to an already loaded week
- **Post-brick running**: Mar 28's T2 run off the long bike — 7 km after 130 km on the bike. These sessions train the specific physiology of running on tired legs that defines Ironman

Volume increased from February and the body handled it. That's the signal you're looking for mid-build: can you do more without breaking?

---

## Strength

Six strength sessions in March — the highest monthly count so far. As total load went up, strength work stayed proportional rather than getting dropped.

Maintaining strength through a hard bike/run block is discipline. It's also what kept injury risk manageable as run volume climbed.

---

## Fitness Metrics at End of Month

Moving from CTL 103 to 119 in one month while keeping TSB near zero (-11.5) is a good result. The body is absorbing load efficiently. At CTL 119, Ironman-specific performance starts to feel attainable.

The near-equal ATL and CTL at month end means March finished without accumulating a significant fatigue deficit. April can build on this without needing a long recovery first.

---

## What's Next

April brings an FTP test on day one — the first structured power benchmark of the year. That result recalibrates all the bike training zones for the final build into Lake Placid. It's also where training shifts from general base and threshold work toward more race-specific intensity. The fitness is there; April is about sharpening it.
