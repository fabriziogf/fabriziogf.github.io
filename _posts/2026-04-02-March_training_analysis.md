## Swim

March's swim stayed in the aerobic lane while the bike and run carried the intensity block — which is correct periodization. Piling Z3/Z4 swim work on top of a hard bike/run phase risks cumulative fatigue without proportional return.

- **A1 and A2 maintenance sets**: the bulk of swim sessions stayed in the aerobic zone — consistent water time without digging a fatigue hole
- **Lap swimming sessions** (mid-week): volume work to keep feel and technique sharp
- **Progressivo A1→A2→A3**: progressive effort sets building through the zones, reinforcing pace sense
- **Sessão Equipamento**: equipment-based sets (paddles, pull buoy) for upper-body strength and stroke mechanics
- **Max effort tests** at month end: 1×200m and 1×400m all-out efforts — the first benchmark tests of the year. These establish calibrated training zones and give a reference point for the rest of the season

The tests at month end are meaningful. Knowing your 200m and 400m times gives you calibrated training zones and tells you where you stand relative to race-pace targets. The fact that they came after a hard March rather than a fresh block makes them conservatively honest.

## Bike

The bike was where the Z3/Z4 block lived:

- **BLOCO Z3>Z4 (semanas 2, 3, 4 de 4)**: weekly interval sessions progressively increasing in duration and intensity across the four-week block
- **Long weekend rides** anchoring volume: 161 km on Mar 7, 162 km on Mar 14, 129 km on Mar 22 — sustained aerobic riding building the endurance base beneath the intervals
- **Força Específica e Zona 3**: specific strength-endurance work on the bike — not just aerobic cruise, but pushing effort in Zone 3 for extended periods
- **Brick combos**: Mar 28 paired a 130 km Z3 bike with an immediate T2 run — the most race-specific session of the month
- **Big gear subidas** (weeks 2, 3, 4): the hill-strength work from February continued, with progressive increases in rep duration each week (7×35s → 7×40s → 7×50s)

The progression in the climbing sessions is worth noting. Going from 6×30s in February to 7×50s in March week 4 represents a meaningful increase in specific muscular load. Lake Placid demands this.

---

## Run

March's running told the clearest story of the month:

- **Long aerobic runs** building to 22–23 km: the Mar 8 "Longo em Pace Fácil" and Mar 15 "Rodando em Ritmo Super Tranquilo" were genuine long runs — not just easy jogs but sustained Z1/Z2 efforts building time on feet
- **Weekly interval progression**: each week's running session pushed slightly harder — from Z2/Z3 combinations toward full Z4 efforts by week 4
- **Meia Chegando** (half marathon coming) on Mar 11: this likely references either a prep session for a half marathon event or the half marathon itself. Either way, it added a harder effort to an already loaded week
- **Post-brick running**: Mar 28's T2 run off the long bike — 7 km after 130 km on the bike. These sessions train the specific physiology of running on tired legs that defines Ironman

The volume increase from February was noticeable, and the body handled it. That's the signal you're looking for mid-build: can you do more without breaking?

---

## Strength

Six strength sessions in March — the highest monthly count so far. The frequency held consistent with the overall training volume increase: as the total load went up, the strength work stayed proportional rather than getting dropped.

Maintaining strength through a hard bike/run block is discipline. It's also the thing that kept the injury risk manageable as run volume climbed.

---

## Fitness Metrics at End of Month

Moving from CTL 103 to 119 in one month while keeping TSB near zero (-11.5) is a good result. The body is absorbing load efficiently. At CTL 119, the fitness is approaching the range where Ironman-specific performance starts to feel attainable.

The near-equal ATL and CTL at month end means March finished without accumulating a significant fatigue deficit. April can build on this without needing a long recovery first.

## What's Next

April brings an FTP test on day one — the first structured power benchmark of the year. That result will recalibrate all the bike training zones for the final build into Lake Placid. It's also where the training starts to shift from general base and threshold work toward more race-specific intensity. The fitness is there; April is about sharpening it.
