title: "February 2026 Training Analysis"
---

February was where training stopped being maintenance and started being construction. January built a floor; February built walls. The sessions got longer, weekly TSS climbed, and by the end of the month the body was carrying real load. A CTL jump from 79 to 103 in four weeks is meaningful. You don't get that from going through the motions.

Ironman Lake Placid is still more than five months away, but July comes faster than you think.

## Swim

February's swim quality stepped up from January. The sessions evolved from pure technique work to structured aerobic sets:

- **20×50m A1–A2 + 20×50m A2**: volume-based sets at controlled intensity, building lactate threshold at the aerobic ceiling
- **Paraquedas + 4×400m**: parachute resistance work for pulling power, then sustained pace sets
- **Natação Recuperativa**: genuine recovery swims, keeping frequency without adding fatigue

The pattern of pairing a harder swim mid-week with an easier one later in the week is good management. February showed more deliberate scheduling than January.

---

## Bike

February's cycling introduced more structure and terrain variation:

- **Long Z2 rides with RPM focus** (3-hour sessions, 120+ km): the Feb 7 and Feb 28 rides anchored the week's volume
- **Big gear climbing work**: the Feb 14 session ("Volume Final com BIG GEAR subidas") — high-torque low-cadence work builds specific bike strength that translates to sustained power on long climbs. Relevant for Lake Placid's hilly bike course
- **Transition to Z3/Z4 block**: the final days of February ("BLOCO Z3>Z4, semana 1 de 4") marked the start of a four-week intensity cycle that would carry into March
- **Brick sessions**: Feb 21 combined a Z2/Z3 bike with a Z3 run immediately after — the first meaningful brick of the year

The big gear sessions are worth highlighting. Lake Placid's bike course has sustained climbs; the ability to push big gears without blowing up is a race-specific skill. Starting to train it in February is the right call.

---

## Run

February's run volume was noticeably higher than January's, and intensity started to creep up:

- **Corrida Levinha** (easy runs, ~13 km): the bread-and-butter aerobic work
- **Progressivo Zona 2 a Zona 3**: controlled progressive efforts — starting easy and finishing at the top of the aerobic zone
- **Após a bike — Corrida Ritmada em Z3**: the Feb 21 brick run was Z3. Running at race-specific effort off the bike, even for 12 km, is meaningful Ironman prep
- **Hill work**: Feb 23 "Subidas (6×30s) + (6×40s)" — short sharp hill repeats for running strength and neuromuscular stimulus

Easy volume plus targeted strength and intensity is the right mix at this stage. Too much intensity too soon is how you get hurt. Too little is how you stagnate.

---

## Strength

Four strength sessions in February, up from two in January. The sessions maintained the same structure — injury prevention work, core activation, compound movements — but more frequency. Consistent strength work across the build phase is the insurance policy against injury when run volume spikes later in spring.

---

## Fitness Metrics at End of Month

The CTL jump from 79 to 103 in one month is exactly what a properly loaded base block produces. The athlete going into March is meaningfully fitter than the one who started February.

The negative TSB (-30.3) reflects the cost. March needs to manage that fatigue carefully — absorbing the February work while continuing to build.

---

## What's Next

March completes the four-week Z3/Z4 intensity block that started at the end of February. The sessions get harder — longer intervals, more specific effort — and the swim sets push toward race-relevant distances. The question is whether the body can absorb the February load and still respond to March's demands. That answer usually shows up in the third week.
